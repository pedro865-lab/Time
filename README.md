1. The Space — Arts & Culture Digital Platform [^0^]
The most prominent "The Space" is a UK-based not-for-profit digital platform dedicated to helping arts, culture, and heritage organizations engage audiences online.
What it does:
 
Funds creation of new digital cultural content
 
Provides free training, mentoring, and online resources for artists and organizations
 
Offers low-cost consultancy for digital content strategy
 
Helps organizations self-publish and monetize digital content (streaming, on-demand video)
Key focus areas:
 
Digital rights management [^1^]
 
Self-publishing platforms for arts organizations (comparing Vimeo OTT, Overture+, TicketCo, etc.) [^2^]
 
Making cultural content accessible to diverse audiences
Funding: Supported by national and regional UK arts agencies
2. Digital Space Ecosystem — European Commission Platform [^3^]
A professional collaboration platform for Europe's space industry (satellites, launch systems, space research).
Features:
 
Domain-focused "Hubs" (digital workspaces) for specific topics
 
Professional matchmaking and partnership building
 
Organization and professional profiles
 
Direct messaging and meeting scheduling
Purpose: Connects public authorities, space agencies, industry, research organizations, and academia across Europe's space sector.
3. Spaces — Virtual Office & Workspace Platform [^4^] 
A global virtual office provider (by IWG/Regus) offering:
 
Prestigious business addresses in thousands of locations worldwide
 
Mail handling and forwarding
 
Professional call answering with live receptionists
 
Access to physical coworking spaces and meeting rooms
 
Business registration services
Not to be confused with "The Space" — this is branded as "Spaces" (plural), but offers virtual workspace solutions.
4. SpaceHey — Retro Social Network [^6^]
A nostalgia-driven social platform (authority level A) that recreates the early 2000s social network experience:
 
Custom HTML/CSS profiles
 
Bulletins, blogs, forums
 
Privacy-friendly (no algorithms, no tracking)
 
Over 2 million users
Not called "The Space" but "SpaceHey" — included here in case you meant this retro platform.
